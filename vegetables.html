Where we get our products

  1. Farmers and Growers
    • We often source a significant portion of their vegetables directly from local farmers and growers. These farmers cultivate a wide variety of vegetables in different regions across the country, ranging from leafy greens and root vegetables to tropical fruits and herbs. We establish direct partnerships with these farmers or purchase vegetables through agricultural cooperatives and wholesalers to ensure freshness and quality.
  2. Markets
    • Wholesale markets play a crucial role in the distribution of vegetables in Sri Lanka. We frequently procure vegetables from wholesale markets located in major cities such as Kandy, and Galle. These wholesale markets act as hubs where farmers, traders, and wholesalers gather to buy and sell large quantities of fresh produce. We send our buyers to these markets to select vegetables based on quality, price, and demand.
  3. Dedicated Suppliers and Distributors
    • We work with dedicated suppliers and distributors specializing in the supply of fresh produce. These suppliers source vegetables from multiple sources, including local farmers, wholesale markets, and importers. They ensure a steady supply of vegetables to supermarkets, often delivering directly to the store's distribution centers or warehouses. These suppliers may also provide additional services such as quality control, packaging, and logistics.
  4. Imported Vegetables
    • While we prioritize locally grown produce, we may also import certain vegetables to meet customer demand and supplement seasonal shortages. Imported vegetables are sourced from international markets and may include items such as broccoli, asparagus, cherry tomatoes, and exotic fruits. We carefully select imported vegetables based on factors such as freshness, quality standards, and import regulations.
